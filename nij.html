Against Nietzsche's Love

"Massage my legs please" says your beloved. Now you sit down beside them, bend forward and start massaging them. You're in a strictly lesser position at this given moment, quite literally serving them. Do you feel disempowered? If so, do the following mental trick: through some silent act or another, remind them of the power you have over them, but more importantly over yourself, through the love they have for you or even better, through the love you have OVER them! You maybe do this by, before bending down to massage them, kissing them, so that they know that indeed this is out of love and not submission. Now you may serve them as much as you like, and it will all be out of your love over them. You don't lose...do you?

On the other hand, at the prospect of massaging your beloved's legs, do you feel empowered? I would argue that's a bit worse. See the last paragraph.

Bowing down in love that you place and almost power over the other person vs Bowing down in love that the other person places on you and powers over you...Nietzsche's love of man overflowing from abundance, coming down from the mountain...To think that loving and compassion can be acts of empowering yourself and placing yourself above the other...

If one is moved by a fear of being disempowered or an affinity of being empowered...If the love of power or the fear of not having it (which amount to the same thing) at bottom is what moves you, then are you really acting out of love? Giving and receiving, quite literally intercourse - the fear at the center of this is then from a perverse overmasculinity. The fear of receiving, of being penetrated. Hegel's master-slave dialectic is enlightening here.

Schenkende Tugend — the gifting virtue, love from abundance — is safe on the face of it. In Nietzsche's own words, there is no object as such but rather it is like a full tree dropping fruit. Ideally, a love from abundance is not reactive, but if I were to make a genealogical guess about where the belief came about in Nietzsche atleast, what with his love of rank, hierarchy and aristocracy, the picture is a bit telling.

Consider this: for the degenerate Nietzschean (as opposed to the model, say), it will always be easier to massage the legs of a beggar on the street than his own wife because he owes the beggar nothing. It is a pure act of coming down. He owes his wife love, and hence the act of massaging may simply be what is owed (see which way the vector points). A love pouring horizontally is rather close to one that could pour back towards you. In a relationship of equals, you too can be given something, you too can be penetrated, and for the degenerate Nietzschean in question, this is being sodomized.

An act of love or compassion that is directed downward (subconsciously it looks this way: I love YOU, I am the lover you are the lovee, I am abundant and love flows out of me to you, I give you, I have the power of love that I am wielding over you...invokes certain Christian strains in my mind) is in the end, only an act of masturbation. The only non-masturbatory act of love is one that (momentarily) forgets the wall between subject and object. Massaging anyone under this light should amount to the same thing, if the object is truly irrelevant. At the ideally practical level, massaging your partner's legs should NOT be a directed act of love, but rather a recognition that this being next to me who I feel one with (do you?) needs something and I can provide it. In fact, I would even place a fetish of leg massages, in which the person doesn't really care whose legs they're massaging, as above the act which stems from directed self-empowering love. If one was to argue that this manner of loving doesn't have anything to do with the actual person, and Eros/Philia has been traded in for Agape, I would counter-argue with: 1) for most of us, oneness-with is a supremely special connection that usually is reserved for our closest few and 2) there is the entire framework of the two-truths doctrine .
