Memento

Mori was a strange boy with strange inclinations. Mori would play strange pranks on Mother and Brother.

When he was 12, he felt like playing dead at dinner. He suddenly flopped his head on the table and let the fork in his hand clatter to the floor. Mother screamed and rushed to check on him. Mori was a silly boy and couldn't keep it together. When Mother was a step away, he burst out laughing. Sitting opposite Mori, Brother too started laughing. Mother did not and Mori was not allowed the rest of the meal. "Worth it.", he said to himself while grinning back to his bedroom with a rumbling stomach.

As he was trying to fall asleep that night, he felt a little disappointed that he couldn't follow through with the prank completely.

That summer, he was out in the local pool with his family. While in the middle of the pool, he decided to try once again. Suddenly, Mori started thrashing his arms and legs violently in the water. For a second he thought he should also yell out for help to really sell the act. He thought it too lacking in subtlety and decided against. As he saw Brother swimming towards him in a panic from the corner of his eye, Mori stopped thrashing just as suddenly and switched to laying completely flat in the water, face down. At the edge of the pool, Mother's face went white. She nearly jumped into the pool herself. Brother swam up to Mori as fast as he could and carried him off to the edge. Mori was completely still. He could feel a little smile try to fight its way out but managed to suppress it. Mother started pressing on his chest to get the water out of his lungs. "Ow", Mori couldn't help. He was just a small boy and Mother's panic-stricken compressions were entirely too much for him. Mother was outraged and slapped Mori very hard. This time, Brother did not laugh but wore a small smile. Mori, on the other hand couldn't stop laughing.

On the completely silent drive home, Mori was somewhat pleased with himself. He just wished he could have held on a little longer.

About two decades passed. In the meantime, Mori never tried that variety of prank again. He didn't want to fail as soon as someone touched him. He'd spent the time getting obscenely muscular and large. He'd also learnt to hold his breath, drop his body's temperature and still his pulse to the point of imperceptibility. At his office job, he spoke to no one. He would clock in and clock out. No one really knew him in this world except Mother and Brother.

"Are you not going to work today?" Mother called out to him at 10 AM. Mori was still in bed. Silence. "Must be tired from the gym", Mother said to herself and went on about her day. At 11:30, he had not moved. Mother came into the room. "Have you told your boss?". She tried to shake him but couldn't. "Such a strange boy." She walked away a little uneasy. When Mori had not moved at all by 6 PM, Mother burst into the room, slamming the door open. "That's enough. Wake up right now."

The evening breeze came in through the window and the wind-chime made a solitary twinkling sound.

Brother came in from work at 7 PM and found Mother weeping on the floor of Mori's room. At around 8:30 PM after the doctor had left, having ruled it a cardiac arrest from the heart being overworked, Mori stood up straight in his bed and said "What's for dinner?".

No one yelled and no one laughed and no one spoke. Mori wore a self-satisfied smirk. Mother and Brother retired to their rooms, having lost their appetite. They weren't sure what to say to Mori or to each other.

A few more years pass. This time, Mori doesn't leave his bed for two full days. Mother and Brother don't cry or shriek. Some worry does set in halfway through the second day, but when Mori wakes up around evening, they simply eat dinner as usual.

Once more and Mori has to piss himself on the fourth day. When he wakes up in a week to a ravenous hunger, Mother tells him he has to change the sheets himself.

Twenty days after the night when Mori dies, aged 47, it is only the smell of the rotting corpse that finally prompts the morticians to get involved. As Mori is carried out into the hearse and Mother stands outside on the drive-way, she spots Brother walking in with a white packet slung from his fingers. Watching the hearse drive away into the night, Mother and Brother sit on the curb and eat the chicken from the packet. It is good.
