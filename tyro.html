A tyro Shaivite says

Shiva dances. Feet of the wind thundering onto icy ground, steps in a circle, pirouette and turn. His eyes are closed and his neck turned up. He sees not whether he has run out of stage. He is All. No Other exists. Shiva is ego-manifest.

In my approximation of Shiva I feel his heedless abandon and his freedom. In the failure of my approximation, I find inside a subtle resentment towards the Other. My stage is cut up and bounded at every turn. Shiva has it easier than me.

To be sure, it is not the external resistance which carves out my world that bothers me. I accept the dues I must pay for being in a world of beings. Once the canals are dug in however, I simply wish to flow through them freely. The stage I speak of is internal. I accept the Other as such but wish to be free of the part of me that constantly checks its unreadable face.

Then, in that grave disgust that bitters my tongue at the notion of being twisted and tossed inside my own mind for the whim of the Other, I discover my pride at last. Who is bigger than me in my universe? Who are any of you to submit me into movement or binding in my own world?

I deem the Other to be worth nothing by way of forced re-indexing. I look down and spit on that unreadable face of the Other for no reason but my own emancipation. Then, the smile breaking through the corner of my mouth, as I dance neither against nor for the pull of the Other but as Shiva dances, is my pride. A proud man, a man who seeks to free himself of the Other, is nothing but a devout Shaivite. This proud man feels a paramount sense of power and relief which he derives from his overcoming of the Other's pull on him. Knowing he is now his own to ruin and exalt, he finally breathes easy and steps surely.

So far, this pride has been the saving grace of my soul.
