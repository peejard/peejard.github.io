A Little Boy

A little boy was brought to the beach for the first time. He went right to the edge of the water and played there for a while. At some point, he decided to make sand castles. "Don't make sand castles near the water. They'll wash away." His mother cried out, laughing to herself at the strangeness of having to make literal a saying.

The kid stared blankly at his mother for a moment and then went on making his sand castle. It looked like the only castle he had ever seen - the bouncy one at a fair some months ago. In some time, a wave softly rushed in and in a quick second of watery push and pull, indeed his bouncy sand castle was a lump of mud.

He stared at the lump on the ground as he sat near the shore. He felt the many little rocks in the sand dig into his knees. He looked out to the horizon and saw nothing but water and clouds above. Quiet for a second, he turned to his mother. She smiled at him. He looked back down to his lump and listened to the water lap at the shore. Again he was quiet for a second.

In some time, he got up and changed spots and started making another bouncy castle. This went on for some time.

In about an hour his mother joined him. Then they played in the water for some time. Then they got back in the car and drove home.
